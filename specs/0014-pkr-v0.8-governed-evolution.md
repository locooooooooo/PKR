# PKR v0.8 Governed Evolution

- Status: Integrated milestone
- Index: [PKR specification index](README.md)
- Scope: Observation, candidate evaluation, promotion, monitoring, and rollback
- Depends on: [PKR v0.7 Persistent Intelligence](0013-pkr-v0.7-persistent-intelligence.md)
- Successor: [PKR v0.9 Compatibility and Release Hardening](0015-pkr-v0.9-release-hardening.md)
- Publication: Integrated into v1.0; not independently released

## 1. Purpose

v0.8 defines how observed project behavior becomes a bounded improvement
candidate without allowing the proposer, evaluator, or Runtime to silently
approve its own authority expansion.

## 2. Candidate lifecycle

A governed evolution MUST follow this order:

1. attributable observations create an Issue and inactive proposal;
2. candidate content receives an immutable digest and declared target;
3. deterministic or external evaluation runs while the candidate is inactive;
4. independent passing Verification is bound to the exact digest;
5. an authorized owner Decision names the evaluated candidate and evidence;
6. promotion records proposer, verifier, approver, promoter, digest, and active
   version;
7. post-promotion monitoring records Metrics against the exact activation;
8. a threshold breach requires rollback and preserves all failure evidence.

Changing candidate content invalidates earlier evaluation and approval
evidence. A failed candidate MUST leave the prior active version unchanged.

## 3. Separation of duties

The proposer MUST NOT self-verify or self-approve a permission, policy, gate,
or capability reduction. The post-promotion observer MUST be independent of the
proposal, verification, approval, and promotion identities. Skill or capability
metadata can describe suitability but cannot grant identity, permission,
approval, or acceptance.

## 4. Protected Runtime boundary

Runtime self-update evidence MUST come from an explicitly external supervisor
and be bound to candidate digest, Runtime version, budgets, protected scope,
and rollback outcome. The authorizing Runtime cannot claim its own activation
or monitoring success. Missing or mismatched evidence fails closed.

## 5. Compatibility role

v0.8 was integrated and accepted for inclusion in v1.0, but it was not a
standalone public release. The accepted v1 contract deliberately classifies
evolution, managed Prompt, Policy, Adapter, Metric, and related direct-store
surfaces as experimental. Milestone acceptance does not promote those surfaces
into the stable compatibility promise.

## 6. Non-goals

v0.8 does not authorize recursive self-modification, autonomous public release,
cross-project model training, or optimization without explicit success and
regression measures.
